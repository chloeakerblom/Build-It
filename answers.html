How do you paint a ceiling without it being rough and streaky?

We are renovating our house at the moment and I can't seem to get a good paint finish. Help!

14 Answers

Make sure to have enough paint on your brush, otherwise there won't be a layer between the brush and the ceiling which will cause it to rub against the paint that is already there. I have made this mistake too many times and I have found that this is always my problem.

Hope that helps!

Listen to the brush. The pile on the brush goes a certain direction which affects the texture of the paint. Always finish with a stroke that goes with the pile (the quieter motion) to get it nice and smooth.

Be gentle with the brush and don't push too hard. Also don't use too much paint or it will get blobby and will drip all over you. It is a nightmare to get ceiling paint out of your clothes. Make sure you always go in the same direction and in straight lines. It is really noticable when your painting is wobbly, particularly near light fittings. And don't forget to use the right paint!
